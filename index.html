NeuroLang is a domain-specific declarative (logic) probabilistic programming language developed by members of the Parietal team of Inria Saclay and NeuroSpin (CEA) in France. It aims to facilitate programming and testing rich cognitive science hypotheses that combine heterogeneous sources of data (a.k.a. modalities).

Presentation of NeuroLang at OHBM 2019 & 2020

Several facets of NeuroLang have been unveiled at OHBM 2019 and 2020.

OHBM 2020 Open Science Room - Lightning Talk

Gaston Zanitti presented how NeuroLang uses ontologies to produce robust forward inference activation patterns from coordinate-based meta-analysis databases.

OHBM posters presenting NeuroLang
